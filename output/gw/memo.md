## 1. Executive Summary

Games Workshop is a vertically integrated designer, manufacturer and retailer of fantasy miniatures and tabletop games, built around its owned Warhammer intellectual property and manufactured in-house in Nottingham [E-001, E-011]. For the 52 weeks ended 1 June 2025, the company delivered the best financial results in its history, with Total Revenue rising to £617.5m and Operating Profit rising to £261.3m, up from £525.7m and £201.8m respectively in the prior period [E-094, E-020, E-021, E-033, E-050]. The investment thesis rests on a uniquely positioned core miniatures business with pricing power and global distribution reach, a fast-growing and highly profitable licensing segment (revenue up from £31.0m to £52.5m) underpinned by the new Amazon media partnership, and strong cash generation supporting reinvestment and shareholder returns [E-045, E-065, E-061]. Trade channel growth of 19.9% and continued store and digital ecosystem expansion further diversify the growth base [E-036, E-008, E-009]. The principal risk flagged by management is that licensing performance, the standout driver of this year's record profit growth, will be very difficult to match in the coming year, alongside a challenging near-term video games licensing market backdrop [E-064, E-085].

## 2. Business Overview

Games Workshop is a vertically integrated business that designs, manufactures, distributes and retails fantasy and science-fiction miniatures and games, built around its owned Warhammer universes, including Warhammer 40,000 and Warhammer Age of Sigmar [E-001, E-004]. The company manages its business centrally from its Nottingham headquarters, where all products are manufactured, employing approximately several thousand staff at the site [E-013, E-011]. Revenue is generated through three complementary sales channels: Retail stores, Trade (independent retailers) and Online [E-005, E-066]. At period end, Games Workshop operated 570 retail stores across 24 countries, split between 424 low-cost format stores and other formats, with 79% of core sales generated outside the UK [E-007, E-002]. The Trade channel comprises approximately 8,100 independent retailer accounts across many countries, having grown by around 900 accounts in the period [E-010]. Beyond physical products, the Black Library imprint sold over 4.5 million novels in the period, and a growing Licensing division monetises the Warhammer IP through video games and new media, with 81% of licensing revenue derived from PC and console games [E-012, E-034]. Digital engagement is an increasingly important operational metric: My Warhammer active users reached approximately 735,000, Warhammer+ subscribers reached approximately 232,000, and email subscribers reached approximately 678,000 at period end [E-008, E-009, E-015]. Store network dynamics during the period included 30 openings (including relocations) against 8 closures, with North America store count increasing from 185 to 201 [E-016, E-017]. The business also continues to invest in manufacturing capacity, with two existing sites (F1 and F2) and Factory 4 under construction, targeted for completion in summer 2026 [E-068, E-069].

## 3. Financial and Operating Performance

Games Workshop reported Total Revenue of £617.5m for the 52 weeks ended 1 June 2025, up from £525.7m in the 53 weeks ended 2 June 2024 [E-020, E-033]. Core revenue (physical products and related sales) was £565.0m, up from £494.7m, while Licensing revenue grew substantially from £31.0m to £52.5m [E-019, E-032, E-045, E-040]. Within Core, Trade revenue on a constant currency basis grew 19.9% to £351.7m, Retail revenue grew 11.3% to £131.3m, and Online revenue was £90.8m, with core revenue at constant currency of £573.8m [E-036, E-035, E-056, E-055, E-057, E-052]. Online sales decreased by 0.1% on a reported basis, though excluding digital sales, direct-to-home online ordering declined by 5.2% [E-037].

Operating Profit rose to £261.3m from £201.8m, with Core operating profit increasing from £174.8m to £211.8m and Licensing operating profit reaching £49.5m [E-021, E-050, E-030, E-049, E-046]. Gross Profit was £445.0m [E-041]. Profit Before Taxation was £262.8m, and Profit Attributable to Owners of the Parent was £196.1m [E-031, E-042, E-026]. Basic Earnings Per Share rose to 594.9p from 458.8p, with Diluted EPS at 593.5p [E-022, E-051, E-059]. A stock provision charge of £7 million was recorded in the period [E-089].

On the balance sheet, Total Equity stood at £280.8m and Cash and Cash Equivalents at £132.6m as at 1 June 2025 [E-060, E-043]. Cash generation was strong: Cash Generated from Operations was £311.5m and Net Cash Generated from Operating Activities was £247.4m, with Capital Expenditure on Property, Plant and Equipment of £24.0m [E-061, E-044, E-062]. The Net increase in cash pre-dividends paid was £197.5m, and the company increased its cash buffer from £80 million to £85 million [E-047, E-101]. Capital returns to shareholders remained substantial, with Dividends per share declared and paid rising to 520.0p from 420.0p, and Dividends Paid to Company Shareholders of £171.4m [E-099, E-103, E-100].

## 4. Value Drivers

- Licensing income has become a material and highly profitable growth driver, with revenue up from £31.0m to £52.5m and operating profit of £49.5m, implying a very high margin relative to the core business [E-045, E-046, E-029].
- Expansion into new media — including the Amazon film/TV partnership and continued exploration of new PC/console/mobile games — represents an additional avenue for IP monetisation alongside the core miniatures business [E-065, E-070, E-063].
- Geographic and channel expansion remains a core driver, evidenced by 19.9% constant-currency Trade revenue growth and plans to open approximately 35 new stores in 2025/26, including entry into new markets [E-036, E-071].
- Pricing power is evident, with average RRP increases of 5% on miniatures and 4% across other product lines, supporting revenue and margin growth alongside volume gains [E-038].
- Growing owned digital engagement (My Warhammer users, Warhammer+ subscribers, email subscribers all increasing) may suggest a strengthening direct audience relationship, though the register does not disclose a specific paid-versus-direct acquisition split [E-008, E-009, E-015].

## 5. Strengths

- Games Workshop delivered the best financial results in its history, with Total Revenue rising from £525.7m to £617.5m and Operating Profit rising from £201.8m to £261.3m, driven by record core sales and licensing income [E-094, E-020, E-033, E-021, E-050].
- The company is a uniquely positioned, vertically integrated business — the only company of its size making fantasy miniatures with a global presence — supported by in-house manufacturing in Nottingham [E-003, E-001, E-011].
- Trade channel growth was particularly strong, with constant-currency revenue growing 19.9% to £351.7m and independent retailer accounts increasing by approximately 900 to about 8,100, broadening distribution at low capital intensity [E-036, E-035, E-010].
- Digital engagement metrics grew strongly, with My Warhammer active users increasing to approximately 735,000 and Warhammer+ subscribers increasing to approximately 232,000 [E-008, E-009].
- Strong balance sheet and cash generation, with Cash Generated from Operations of £311.5m and a cash buffer increased from £80m to £85m, support continued dividend payouts and reinvestment [E-061, E-101].

## 6. Material Risks

### Operational

Games Workshop faces ongoing stock availability challenges across core IP ranges, requiring continued operational work to meet demand, compounded by global supply chain disruption and instability that may negatively impact manufacturing [E-090, E-088]. Separately, the viability of licensing deals depends on partners' successful delivery, largely outside the company's control [E-091].

### IT systems and cyber security

Cyber attacks could cause reputational damage, regulatory fines, operational disruption, IP leaks and negative financial impact, with an increasing risk trend flagged [E-087]. Failure to ensure continuous supply from key manufacturing and warehousing facilities, including due to IT systems failure, could impair the company's ability to supply customers [E-092].

### Brand and reputation

Failure to protect intellectual property may erode competitive advantage and undermine reputation, negatively impacting financial performance, with an increasing risk trend disclosed [E-086]. This is particularly material given the Warhammer universes represent the core value driver underpinning both the core business and the licensing segment [E-086].

### Climate change and environmental obligations

Short to medium-term climate risks include physical impacts from extreme weather affecting supply chain and manufacturing, alongside transitional risks such as carbon or fossil fuel taxation increasing costs [E-093]. These risks are monitored against a backdrop of dependence on concentrated manufacturing and warehousing facilities [E-018].

### Analytical observations

It appears that the concentration of manufacturing and warehousing in a small number of sites (Nottingham, Memphis) is not disclosed as a standalone risk category but recurs across supply chain, climate and IT risk disclosures, which may suggest a single-region disruption could have an outsized impact on group-wide fulfilment [E-018, E-088, E-092]. Additionally, EPS growth from 458.8p to 594.9p has only marginally outpaced Operating profit growth from £201.8m to £261.3m, and the gap between Profit Attributable to Owners of £196.1m and the reported per-share figures implies share count reduction is a meaningful lever; this may suggest future EPS growth could decouple from underlying operating growth if buyback activity slows or reverses [E-022, E-051, E-028, E-026].

### Items requiring further investigation

Management has explicitly flagged that licensing performance, a major driver of this year's record results, will be very difficult to match next year, with the video games licensing market backdrop remaining challenging in the short term [E-064, E-085]. Further diligence is needed on the specific terms and durability of licensing contracts underpinning this segment [E-064, E-085].

## 7. Bull and Bear Cases

### Bull case

- Record FY25 results, with Total Revenue of £617.5m and Operating Profit of £261.3m, demonstrate the scalability of the core business and the emergence of an additive, high-margin licensing segment [E-024, E-025, E-023].
- Balanced growth across Trade (+19.9%) and Retail (+11.3%), alongside continued investment of £20.1m in the Warhammer Studio, supports management's stated strategy of growing all channels in harmony [E-036, E-056, E-102, E-066].
- New media optionality, including the Amazon partnership and an expanding video game slate, could unlock further licensing income with limited capital outlay, as licensees typically bear production costs [E-065, E-078].
- A strong balance sheet, with Total Equity of £280.8m and Cash Generated from Operations of £311.5m, provides capacity to fund Factory 4 construction and European warehouse expansion alongside continued dividends [E-060, E-061, E-069, E-081].

### Bear case

- Management has signalled that licensing performance, the standout driver of FY25's record profit growth, will be very difficult to match in FY26, creating risk of growth deceleration from an elevated base [E-064, E-085].
- The gap between Profit Attributable to Owners of £196.1m and Basic EPS growth to 594.9p suggests share count reduction is a meaningful driver of per-share growth; this may suggest EPS growth could fall below operating profit growth if buybacks slow [E-022, E-026, E-100].
- Online sales excluding digital declined, driven by fewer customers ordering directly from home, which may suggest a structural shift toward in-store and Trade ordering that could affect online channel economics [E-037].
- Concentration in a small number of manufacturing hubs (Nottingham, Memphis) exposes growth ambitions to single-point-of-failure risk from supply chain, IT, or climate-related disruption [E-018, E-088, E-092].

2. What are the specific financial terms (minimum guarantees, royalty rates, duration) of the Amazon partnership and other licensing deals, given management flagged that FY25's licensing performance will be very difficult to repeat? [E-065, E-064]
3. What is driving the 5.2% decline in direct-to-home online ordering excluding digital sales, and is this a permanent structural shift toward in-store/Trade fulfilment or a temporary effect? [E-037]
